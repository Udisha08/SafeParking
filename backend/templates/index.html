Decision Tree Model

Decision trees use multiple algorithms to decide to split a node into two or more sub-nodes. Purity of the node increases with respect to the target variable. The decision tree splits the nodes on all available variables and then selects the split which results in most homogeneous sub-nodes.

  • Selection of best spot

    Parking spots with more cameras and less crime in the area will give the best rank.

  • Drawbacks of using Decision Tree

    Though we got satisfactory results, we wanted a more stable model for the predictive analysis.

Random Forest Classfier

Random Forest gave us an prediction with an apprximate accuracy rate of 85%. Thus, we use this classification method as our model to rank the parking spot suggesting increased crime rates in open spots offering overnight parking.

Advantage - Random Forest is more robust and accurate than decision trees.
